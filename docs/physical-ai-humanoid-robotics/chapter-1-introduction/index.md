## What is Physical AI?

Physical AI refers to the integration of artificial intelligence with physical systems, particularly robotic platforms that interact with the real world. Unlike traditional AI that operates in digital spaces, Physical AI systems must navigate, manipulate, and understand three-dimensional environments.

### Key Characteristics

- **Embodiment**: The AI system has a physical form that interacts with the environment
- **Real-time Processing**: Must respond to environmental changes in real-time
- **Sensorimotor Integration**: Combines sensory input with motor output
- **Environmental Adaptation**: Adjusts behavior based on physical context

## Embodied Intelligence Principles

Embodied intelligence is a theory that suggests intelligence emerges from the interaction between an agent and its environment. This approach contrasts with traditional AI that focuses on abstract reasoning.

### Core Principles

1. **Embodiment**: Intelligence is shaped by the physical form of the agent
2. **Emergence**: Complex behaviors emerge from simple interactions
3. **Environment Coupling**: The environment plays a role in cognition
4. **Morphological Computation**: The body contributes to computation

## Humanoid Robotics Overview

Humanoid robots are designed with human-like characteristics, including bipedal locomotion, manipulator arms, and often a head with sensors positioned similarly to human senses.

### Components of Humanoid Robots

- **Locomotion System**: Legs and balance control for walking
- **Manipulation System**: Arms and hands for object interaction
- **Perception System**: Cameras, microphones, and other sensors
- **Cognitive System**: Processing units for decision making
- **Communication System**: Interfaces for human interaction

## AI-Robot Interaction Models

Effective Physical AI systems require sophisticated models for understanding and interacting with their environment and users.

### Interaction Types

- **Perception-Action Loops**: Continuous sensing and response cycles
- **Learning from Demonstration**: Imitation learning from human examples
- **Natural Language Interaction**: Voice and text-based communication
- **Social Interaction**: Understanding human social cues and norms

## System Architecture Patterns

Physical AI systems typically follow specific architectural patterns that enable effective integration of perception, cognition, and action.

### Common Patterns

- **Subsumption Architecture**: Layered control systems
- **Behavior-Based Robotics**: Decomposition into behavioral modules
- **Hybrid Deliberative/Reactive**: Combination of planning and reactive systems
